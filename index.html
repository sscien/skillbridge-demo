QUESTIONS

Fair questions, straight answers

How can clients trust me if they can't see my portfolio?

Three platform-backed signals cross the wall for you: your tier (earned reputation), your certifications (platform-graded skill assessments with scores), and SkillBridge's QA guarantee on every delivery. Clients trust the platform's word — that's the product.

How and when do I get paid?

Clients pay into escrow before work begins. After your delivery passes staff QA, the payout releases — you keep 80%. Time-bound licenses renew into repeat payouts; everything appears on your monthly royalty statement.

Who owns my work?

You do, always. Work is licensed, never sold. Files stay in your encrypted vault until a license is signed and paid, and every deliverable carries an invisible per-license fingerprint so leaks are traceable and enforceable.

How do I judge quality if I can't see who made it?

You see what predicts quality: a verified tier, certification scores, a watermarked preview excerpt, and a staff QA report on delivery — all backed by the rematch-or-refund guarantee. What you don't get is a name to bias you.

What if the delivery is bad?

The SkillBridge Guarantee: every delivery passes a staff QA checklist before you see it, and if it still fails you, a dispute freezes the escrowed funds until a human resolves it — rematch or refund.

Why can't I just browse the talent?

Browsing is how prices race to the bottom and how talent gets poached, copied, and burned. Curation is how you get the right match instead of the loudest bidder. The wall protects both sides — including you.

What does it cost?

Joining is free on both sides. Clients pay the quoted proposal price — no hidden fees. The platform keeps 20% of each transaction, which funds vetting, valuation, QA, escrow, and IP protection.

What stops us from just working together directly?

The terms prohibit it — but more practically, going direct means losing escrow, QA, the guarantee, licensing enforcement, and the next match. The wall isn't a cage; it's the service.

Where does the 20% fee actually go — is it added on top?

Nothing is added on top. The price you approve is the full price. Example: a proposal says $5,000 — you pay exactly $5,000; the talent receives $4,000 (80%) and SkillBridge keeps $1,000 (20%), which funds the vetting, escrow, human QA, and the rematch-or-refund guarantee.

Can I talk to the talent, or own the work outright?

For creative work you can request one live working session per milestone — hosted by us, voice-masked, and only with the talent's consent. And if a piece was commissioned just for you, ask about full ownership: bespoke commissions have a lower assignment floor (1.75× the license price) than catalog works (4×).
