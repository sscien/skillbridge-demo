How the wall works

You can't inspect the person.
So inspect the machine.

Marketplaces ask you to trust a stranger's profile. We ask you to trust a process you can read, step by step — the same five gates, every single transaction.

1 · Vet

Clients prove identity and declare intended use before they can transact. Talent registers skills with evidence and takes platform-graded assessments. Nobody unvetted crosses the front door.

2 · Match

A human matcher — assisted by a transparent scoring model, never replaced by it — pairs the need with the right vault work. Both briefs are rewritten in the platform's voice; a stylometry co-pilot flags anything that could identify a person.

3 · Escrow

The client funds a milestone before work begins. Money sits with the platform — talent knows it's real, clients know it's recoverable.

4 · QA

Every deliverable passes a staff checklist before the client sees it: scope match, zero identity leakage, docs included. The QA report ships with the delivery.

5 · Guarantee

Rematch or refund if delivery fails. Disputes freeze funds until a human resolves them. Every delivery carries an invisible per-license fingerprint, so leaks are traceable and enforceable.

INTEGRITY BY CONSTRUCTION

The wall isn't a policy. It's architecture.

Row-level security

From the first database migration, each side's data is unreadable by the other — enforced by the database itself, not by application politeness.

Column-level grants

Even on records both sides share (like a license), the columns naming the other party are unreadable. The wall exists inside single rows.

Audited crossings

Platform staff can see both sides — and every such access is written to an append-only audit log. The wall has a black box.

One voice

All messages relay through the platform. An identity scrubber catches emails, phones, and handles before a human moderator relays a word.
